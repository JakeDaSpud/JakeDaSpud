hobbies

I've always loved learning things, and that's one of the reasons I love game development. Games as a medium allow me to learn anything I want to, and still be relevant because I can put them into a project.

Since 2020 I've played guitar and have quite a few by now, I used to take piano lessons but I never got into theory, although I still love sound technology and music.

I love learning languages, I am native in English, I can barely speak Irish (but I want to re-learn it and study the culture more), I've taken Spanish for 8 years while casually speaking it, and I learned German when I lived there for 6 months, and have loved learning it since.

I didn't try programming until I went to college, but I had always assumed I'd like it. I tried learning Python from a magazine before college, but it was explained terribly and I didn't even know what for i in range(5) was doing: where did i come from? WHAT IS i?? I understood everything from my first language's module: Java, a statically typed language (something I look for in all my languages now!).

On the note of programming, I do like dabbling in lower-levels, 6502 Assembly up to C are interesting when looking at optimisation and learning how CPUs / Computers work, I also took a liking to Cryptography this way. Abstracting to human-readable code isn't that different from decrypting messages!

My gaming taste has changed quite a bit over my life. I started as a PS3 trickshotter during my CoD:AW and BO3 days, but now I casually play Valorant with friends.
